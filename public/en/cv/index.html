Curriculum Vitae

Tobias Hodel | Translations:
  • De

critically shaping the digital (r)evolution

Research Interests#

Digital Humanities, application and theory of machine learning in the humanities, digital history, epistemology and theory of Digital Humanities, editing and archiving techniques, media and media theory (pre-modern and post-modern), history of record-keeping, digital teaching methods, pre-modern administration, auxiliary sciences of history.

Education#

2024 — Habilitation (cumulative) in Digital Humanities and Auxiliary Sciences of History: “Texterkennung und Textanalyse: Methodische Evolution durch maschinelle Lernverfahren”, University of Bern. Defended 8 November 2024.

2016 — PhD in History: “Schriftordnungen im Wandel. Produktions-, Gebrauchs- und Aufbewahrungspraktiken von klösterlichem Schriftgut in Königsfelden (1300–1600)”, University of Zurich. Supervised by Prof. Dr. Simon Teuscher (UZH) & Prof. Dr. Adam Kosto (Columbia University). Defended 11 November 2016 (summa cum laude). Published 2020.

2010 — Licentiatus Philosophiae (lic. phil.) in General History and German Literature, University of Zurich.

Positions#

2025 — Application for promotion to Associate Professor by the Faculty of Humanities.

2020 — Call for W2 Professorship in Digital History (declined). University of Bamberg.

Since 2019 — Assistant Professor (tenure track) in Digital Humanities, University of Bern. Positive interim evaluations 2021 and 2023.

2017–2019 — Project leader, digital edition of the Königsfelden monastery records (1300–1656), University of Zurich.

2016–2019 — Research associate, READ project (Recognition and Enrichment of Archival Documents), State Archives of Zurich.

2015–2019 — Project leader, Ad fontes (introduction to working with historical sources), University of Zurich.

2012–2016 — PhD candidate, SNSF project “Königsfelden und sein Adel”, University of Zurich.

2002–2010 — Professional handball goalkeeper (part-time), including Swiss national team.
