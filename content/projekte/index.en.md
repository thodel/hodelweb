Current and completed projects at Digital Humanities, University of Bern. Full details on the [DH Bern projects page](https://dhbern.github.io/content/projects/).

---

### The Flow
Historical research increasingly benefits from digitisation, Handwritten Text Recognition, and Natural Language Processing.
→ [Learn more](https://dhbern.github.io/content/projects/flow/index.html)

### Economies of Space
Using the example of Basel, this project researches urban real estate management and the related economic discourses, practices, and actors.
→ [Learn more](https://dhbern.github.io/content/projects/economies-of-space/index.html)

### Confoederatio Ludens
CH LUDENS examines a vibrant game design and development scene in pre-Internet Switzerland.
→ [Learn more](https://dhbern.github.io/content/projects/ch-ludens/index.html)

### Bullinger digital
Handwriting models for automatic recognition of Heinrich Bullinger's letters, in cooperation with the University of Zurich and HES-SO.
→ [Learn more](https://dhbern.github.io/content/projects/bullinger-digital/index.html)

### BeNASch
Establishing a standard for projects working with pre-modern German texts.
→ [Learn more](https://dhbern.github.io/content/projects/benasch/index.html)

### Bit Philology
Literary archives increasingly include born-digital documents. This project explores how to make them accessible.
→ [Learn more](https://dhbern.github.io/content/projects/bitphilology/index.html)

### Bundesratsprotokolle
Processing the handwritten Swiss Federal Council minutes (1848–1903) for accessible full-text search.
→ [Learn more](https://dhbern.github.io/content/projects/aufbereitung-bundesratsprotokolle/index.html)

### Discrimination-Sensitive Metadata Practice
A practical guide for discrimination-sensitive description of metadata for historical sources.
→ [Learn more](https://dhbern.github.io/content/projects/metadata-handbook/index.html)

### GeWiS – Grounded Knowledge
Investigating how relational databases and linked data approaches can be combined to model and interlink knowledge from the humanities.
→ [Learn more](https://dhbern.github.io/content/projects/gewis/index.html)

### Text Generator for Style Imitation
How can machine-learning language models generate short texts that approximate the writing style of specific authors?
→ [Learn more](https://dhbern.github.io/content/projects/walser-hennings-generator/index.html)
